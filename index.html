That said, while you definitely want to tell an engaging story on your site, you’re not writing your autobiography here! Just like hiring managers don’t spend much time on your resume, there’s a good chance that the majority of people visiting your personal website aren’t going to spend hours clicking around. So keep it short and snappy—people will get much more from two well-written paragraphs than they will from skimming over two pages, I promise. Right off the bat, when people land on your site, you want them to understand who you are, what you do, and what you’re looking for. So, somewhere that’s easily accessible (ideally your home page or “about” page), you should include a summary—no more than two to three paragraphs long—laying out the most important things about you. This could include major companies you’ve worked for, projects you’ve worked on that you’re most proud of, your “superpower” as a professional, and even a little personality.
